  • The cleaner your solar panels are, the more energy they produce. It is generally agreed upon that dirty and soiled panels can lose up to 20% of renewable energy production. Most solar panels require at least annual cleaning, though cleaning them more often is not uncommon. Remember that solar panels are electrically charged and naturally attract dust, therefore the general rule of solar panel maintenance is to have them cleaned and inspected every six months. Solar panel cleaning frequency also depends on other factors such as the natural environment. Are they located in an area that's windy and dusty? Are they located next to a freeway or airport? Are there pigeons on the roof or under the solar panels making a mess on them? And even the slope of the roof can have a dramatic affect on cleanliness and production. Are they installed on a high-pitched roof? Or are they installed on a flatter low-pitched roof? The flatter the roof, the faster they get dirty.

  • There is a well established misconception that solar panel cleaning is unnecessary because the rain will clean them. This is false. Have you ever owned a car, especially a dark colored one? You know first hand how quickly these dark painted surfaces accumulate dirt and grime very quickly. The dark glass of the solar panels are similar. Just in the way that rain never cleaned a dark colored car, the rain will also not clean solar panels. Why? This is because just like a car, the glass of the solar panel needs to be agitated and routinely scrubbed off before these surfaces will get clean once again. When you hire the licensed and insured Affordable Solar Cleaning Pros to clean your panels you can rest assured that we will never use any harsh chemicals or detergents to clean your panels, potentially voiding their warranty. We only use professional solar panel cleaning brushes which will scrub and agitate the debris away cleansing and rinsing the panels with our pure mineral free water created by our deionizing water filtration system.
